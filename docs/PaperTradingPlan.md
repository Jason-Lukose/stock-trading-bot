# Paper Trading Plan

## Framing Correction

The PDF sells "zero screen time" from day one. That framing is rejected for the MVP. Paper trading is an actively monitored validation phase whose purpose is to find the ways the system breaks — not passive income theater. Automation of oversight comes only after the system has earned trust.

The PDF's "at least 2 weeks" is also insufficient: for the 4-hr trend strategy, two weeks may contain zero signals. Duration below is per-strategy and evidence-based.

## Entry Criteria (all required)

- Backtester complete, all tests green
- Strategy passed the Minimum Evidence Gate in Backtesting.md (per strategy — strategies enter paper individually, not as a bundle)
- Risk manager tests green, including ceilings, halts, and kill switch
- Reconciliation-on-startup implemented and tested
- Runbook (OperationsRunbook.md) reviewed

## Phases

**Phase A — Shadow mode (1 week).** Bot runs live against paper account but with order submission disabled: signals, risk decisions, and would-be orders are logged only. Verifies data feed, scheduling, calendar handling, and signal timing with zero execution risk.

> **Amendment (D-030, post-Phase 4):** none of the current strategies passed the Minimum Evidence Gate (Backtesting.md) — see D-029. The Entry Criteria above gate *execution* phases (B/C) on a strategy passing that gate; Phase A submits no orders, so it is safe and useful to run the current (research-only, evidence-gate-failing) strategies through Phase A anyway, purely to validate the end-to-end pipeline (data → signal → risk decision → logged would-be order) with zero execution risk. No strategy proceeds to Phase B or C until it independently passes the evidence gate.

**Phase B — Single instrument (2+ weeks).** Enable the single strategy with the most backtest trades (likely SPY mean reversion). Daily human log review.

**Phase C — Full portfolio (4+ weeks or ≥ 30 combined trades, whichever is longer).** All gated strategies enabled. Correlation filter behavior observed in practice.

A phase restarts if a code change touches strategy, risk, or execution logic.

## Daily Monitoring Checklist (human, ~10 min)

- Every trade in `trades.csv` traceable to a logged signal + risk decision (no orphan orders)
- Position sizes match ATR math (spot-check one trade)
- Stops attached at entry, never widened
- No `RISK_HALT_*` events, or if present, they behaved correctly
- Local state matches Alpaca dashboard (reconciliation clean)
- Data staleness warnings: zero, or explained

## Divergence Tracking (the core deliverable)

Weekly, compare paper results to backtest expectations for the same period: fill quality vs modeled slippage, signal count vs expected frequency, win rate vs backtest band, and P&L trajectory. Material divergence (e.g., realized slippage > 2× modeled) is a finding that must be fed back into the backtester's cost model — after which affected strategies re-run the evidence gate.

## Success / Failure Criteria per Strategy

**Pass:** ≥ 30 paper trades, behavior consistent with backtest (signal frequency, win rate within band, slippage within 2× model), zero unexplained orders, zero reconciliation mismatches, drawdown within limits.

**Fail (strategy disabled, back to research):** performance outside backtest band with ≥ 30 trades, any risk rule observed not enforcing, or persistent data/execution errors.

## What Comes After

Nothing automatic. Completing paper trading successfully produces a written review and a decision. Live trading remains out of scope for the MVP, and the PDT precondition (RiskRules.md Rule 10) plus a full go/no-go review stand between this plan and any live consideration.

## Daily Briefings

Morning/evening summaries (per the PDF's Step 4 concept) are a fine reporting layer and will be generated by `reporting/daily_report.py` from logs. They supplement the daily checklist during paper trading; they replace it only after Phase C passes.
